There was a time when sharing meant just... sharing. You had tabs open, your friend needed them, you sent them over. Done.

No account. No cloud sync. No "upgrade to share with more than 3 people." Just links, passed from one browser to another.

Things have changed. And honestly, not all of it is bad. Session managers exist, bookmark syncing works, and browser profiles keep your work and life separate. These are real problems that deserve real solutions.

But I also think some tools should just exist. A way to save your tabs, share them with someone, and not worry about where that data goes. No server. No tracking. No account. Your tabs stay in your browser. When you share, the data lives in the link itself — nowhere else.

Not everything needs to be a business. Some things can just exist.

So this one's free.

Privacy

Sesh doesn't store your sessions on any server. When you share, all the data is compressed and encoded directly into the URL — the link IS the data. No server ever sees your tabs. Nothing leaves your browser unless you choose to share. The source code is open for anyone to verify.
